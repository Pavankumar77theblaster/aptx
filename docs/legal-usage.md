# APT-X Legal Usage Guidelines

## Authorization Requirements

**APT-X must only be used against systems you have explicit, written authorization to test.**

### Before Testing

1. Obtain written authorization from the system owner
2. Define clear scope boundaries
3. Document emergency contacts
4. Understand legal implications in your jurisdiction

### Authorization Documentation

Your authorization should include:
- Scope definition (IP ranges, domains)
- Testing timeframe
- Allowed test types
- Out-of-scope systems
- Point of contact
- Emergency procedures

## Prohibited Activities

**Do NOT use APT-X for:**

- Unauthorized access to any system
- Testing systems without explicit permission
- Denial of service attacks
- Data theft or exfiltration
- Malicious exploitation
- Testing third-party systems via authorized targets

## Legal Compliance

### United States
- Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
- State-specific computer crime laws
- Industry regulations (PCI-DSS, HIPAA)

### European Union
- GDPR data protection requirements
- National cybercrime laws
- EU Cybersecurity Act

### Other Jurisdictions
- Consult local legal counsel
- Understand cross-border implications

## Best Practices

1. **Document Everything**: Maintain logs of authorization and activities
2. **Minimize Impact**: Use safe mode, rate limiting
3. **Report Responsibly**: Follow coordinated disclosure
4. **Protect Data**: Handle findings securely
5. **Know Your Limits**: Stay within authorized scope
